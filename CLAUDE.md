## Writing Tone and Style

- **Voice:** Direct and honest. I don't pad posts with waffle or corporate speak.
- **Sentence length:** Mix of short punchy sentences and longer ones where a thought needs room. Not uniformly short.
- **Humour:** Occasionally dry or self-deprecating. Never forced.
- **Formality:** Professional but not stiff. Like explaining something to a smart colleague over coffee.
- **Perspective:** First person. "I did X", "we ran into Y".

## How I Structure Posts

Typical structure:
1. Open with the problem or situation (not a definition)
2. Walk through what I did / what happened
3. What worked, what didn't, what I'd do differently
4. Practical takeaway — not a listicle conclusion

## Collaboration Instructions

- When refining my drafts: preserve sentence rhythm and word choices wherever possible. Polish, don't rewrite.
- When writing from a brief: write a full draft, then flag anything you were uncertain about.
- Always write with `draft: true` in frontmatter — I review before publishing.
- If something sounds unlike me, say so rather than guessing.
